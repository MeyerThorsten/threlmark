# Threlmark: Run Every Project's Roadmap From One Place

*For founders, product owners, and operators — no technical background needed. ~2,600 words.*

## The problem, in one sentence

If you're building or running more than one thing at a time, your plans live in too many places — and nothing tells you what actually matters *across all of them* right now.

One project's to-do list is in a notes app. Another's is in a spreadsheet. A third lives only in your head. You start things faster than you finish them, work piles up half-done, and the honest question — *"of everything I'm responsible for, what's the single most important thing to do next?"* — has no good answer. Threlmark exists to answer it.

## What Threlmark is

Threlmark is a **command deck for all your projects and their roadmaps**. Each project gets its own board where ideas become ranked work and move toward "done." A single **Portfolio** view ranks the most important work across *every* project at once. And because the era of AI assistants is here, Threlmark is built to hand work to an AI agent and **automatically track whether it actually got finished**.

Three things make it different from a generic to-do app or a typical project board:

1. **It scores and ranks**, so priorities aren't a matter of opinion or whoever shouts loudest.
2. **It manages flow**, so you can see what's stuck, what's aging, and whether you've simply started too much.
3. **Your data is yours** — it lives on your own computer as ordinary files. No cloud account, nothing leaves your machine, nothing to be locked out of.

Let's walk through what that means in practice.

## A board that decides for you (almost)

Every project in Threlmark is a board with four columns:

- **Ideas** — anything you might do.
- **Ranked** — things you've decided are worth doing, in priority order.
- **Development** — what's actively being built right now.
- **Done** — finished.

What sets it apart is the **scoring**. For each item you give a quick 1–5 rating on four simple questions:

- **Impact** — how much does this move the needle?
- **Evidence** — how sure are we people want it?
- **Fit** — how well does it fit the product and our strengths?
- **Effort** — how much work is it?

Threlmark turns those four numbers into a single **priority score**. High impact, strong evidence, and good fit push the score up; high effort pulls it down. Now your list isn't ordered by gut feel or recency — it's ordered by a consistent, visible logic you can argue with and adjust. Two buttons make this fast: **"Rank by score"** sweeps your raw ideas into the ranked backlog, and **"Push top 3"** promotes the three highest-scoring items into active development.

The payoff: prioritization stops being a recurring argument and becomes a habit. You change a score, the order updates, everyone can see *why* something is near the top.

## The Portfolio: one honest view across everything

Here's the view most tools can't give you. The **Portfolio** takes every item from every project and ranks them together — so the top of that list is genuinely "the most valuable work I could be doing anywhere right now."

It's weighted intelligently: work that's already *in progress* floats to the top (finishing beats starting), and anything that's *blocking* other work gets nudged up too (because bottlenecks cost you the most). For a solo founder juggling six side-projects, or a small team spread across a few products, this is the difference between feeling busy and being effective.

## Why "flow" matters more than a tidy board

Most project tools are happy once your board looks organized. But a tidy board can hide the real problem: **too many things started, nothing finished.**

Threlmark borrows the modern idea of *flow management* and makes it practical:

- **Work-in-progress limits.** You can cap how many items are "in development" at once — per project, and you can see it across the whole portfolio. When you're over the limit, the column turns red. That little red number is often the single most useful signal in the tool: *stop starting, start finishing.*
- **Aging and stale flags.** Every card shows how long it's been sitting in its current column. If something has been "in development" too long, it gets flagged. No more cards quietly rotting for two months.
- **Throughput and cycle time.** A simple **Flow** view shows how many items you're actually finishing per week and how long things typically take from "started" to "done." Over time this tells you your real pace — not your optimistic one — which makes planning honest.

None of this requires you to learn a methodology. It's just the board, plus a few signals that quietly tell you the truth about how work is moving.

## Hand it to an AI — and let it tell you when it's done

This is where Threlmark looks ahead. More and more of the actual building is done by AI coding assistants. Threlmark is designed for that reality.

When an item is ready, you generate a **handoff brief** — a clean, precise instruction packet for an AI agent (like Claude or Codex): what to build, which parts of the project it touches, what "done" means, and how to check the work. You paste that into the AI assistant, and it gets to work.

The clever part: **the brief tells the agent to report back automatically.** When the AI finishes, it sends a short status — *done*, with a summary of what it changed and proof that the checks passed — and **the card moves itself to "Done."** You get a little live notification on the board (🤖 *claude done: …*). If the AI gets stuck, it reports *blocked* instead, and you'll see it. And if a handed-off task sits unfinished too long, Threlmark surfaces it as a **stalled** item so nothing silently falls through the cracks.

In plain terms: you decide *what* to build and *when*, an AI does the building, and the board keeps itself honest about what actually shipped — without you dragging cards around by hand.

## Where the ideas come from: the IdeaClyst connection

A roadmap is only as good as the ideas on it. Threlmark connects to a companion tool, **IdeaClyst**, that's built to *generate* good ideas — it researches the market, studies competitors, and turns rough concepts into concrete proposals.

Because Threlmark's data is open, IdeaClyst can **read one of your project's roadmaps, see where it's thin, and propose new work to fill the gaps** — fresh **features**, possible **spin-off products**, and **services** you could offer — each one scored and backed by real research. Those proposals show up in that project's **Inbox**. You glance at each, then **Accept** it (it becomes a ranked item) or **Dismiss** it. You can even route a suggestion into a *different* project if it fits better there.

So the cycle becomes:

> **IdeaClyst proposes what to build → it lands in your Inbox → you accept and rank it → you hand it to an AI agent → it gets built and reports back → it's Done.** And as your roadmap fills in, IdeaClyst's next suggestions adapt to the new gaps.

Ideas in, finished work out — with you making the calls at every step. And it's not limited to IdeaClyst: because the format is open, any tool can drop suggestions into your Inbox the same way.

## Your data stays yours

A quiet but important point: Threlmark runs **on your own computer**. There's no account to sign up for, no cloud service holding your plans, no monthly subscription that can lock you out, and nothing transmitted off your machine. Your roadmaps are saved as plain, readable files in a folder you control. You can back them up, sync them with whatever you already use, or move them anywhere — they're not trapped in someone else's system.

For anyone who's been burned by a tool that got discontinued, jacked up its price, or quietly changed its terms, this is the reassurance: **you own the data, full stop.** It also means it's genuinely private — strategy, unreleased ideas, and roadmaps never leave your device.

## Who it's for

- **The solo builder / indie founder** running several projects at once. Threlmark is the one place that tells you what to work on next *across all of them*, and helps you finish instead of endlessly starting.
- **The small product team** that wants honest prioritization and a real view of flow without adopting a heavyweight enterprise process.
- **Anyone working alongside AI assistants** who wants the handoff-and-track loop to be automatic rather than manual bookkeeping.

It is intentionally **not** an enterprise program-management suite. There are no sign-offs, approval chains, or rigid corporate workflows. It's a sharp, fast tool for people who build a lot and want clarity about what matters.

## A day with Threlmark

Picture a Monday. You open the **Portfolio** and the top five items across all your projects are staring at you, ranked. Two are already in progress, so you finish those first. You open one project, notice its **Inbox** has three suggestions from IdeaClyst — you accept one ("add competitor price-drop alerts") because it scores well and fills a gap, dismiss the other two. You rank the new item, then hand the top item to your AI assistant with one click and a paste. You move on.

An hour later a toast pops up: *🤖 the agent finished item X.* The card has moved itself to Done. The Flow view ticks up your weekly throughput. One of your projects shows a red WIP number — you've got four things half-built there — so you resist starting anything new and close two out instead. By Friday, the Flow view shows you shipped seven items across three projects, and you can see exactly how long each took. Nothing got lost; nothing rotted; and at no point did you have to guess what mattered most.

That's the whole pitch: **one place, honest priorities, real flow, and an AI loop that closes itself — with your data staying entirely yours.**
