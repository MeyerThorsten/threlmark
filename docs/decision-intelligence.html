Decision intelligence,
for every vertical

Threlmark already recorded everything — transitions, due dates, handoffs, decisions, outcomes. The Insights layer turns that record into answers, and vertical templates + free-form categories open the same engine to marketing teams, trading desks, ops and compliance — not just software.

04

Outcome ledger

What shipped and what it produced — the honest changelog.

The risk register

A triage list, not a dashboard ornament: what is wrong, how bad, and what to do next.

SignalTriggerSeverity
overduepast its due date, not Donehigh

Each run draws random weeks from the real history until the backlog hits zero. Zero-throughput weeks are part of the distribution — that is what makes the P85 honest.

Two honesty rules. Fewer than 2 non-zero weeks → no forecast, with the reason stated. And the PRNG is seeded (mulberry32), so the same board always produces the same dates.

Verticals: same engine, your vocabulary

Five templates seed categories, WIP limits and lane policies at creation. Item categories are free-form strings — an external tool’s "Campaigns" or "CAPA" now round-trips intact instead of being coerced to "Build".

Software product — the classic board
Research · Discovery · Reports · Trends · Validation · Build · Distribution · Operations · UX · Automation
Marketing & content — Done = published
Content · Campaigns · SEO · Social · Email · Video · Brand · Partnerships · Analytics
Business operations — one owner per item
Strategy · Finance · Hiring · Process · Legal · Vendors · Sales · Support · Tooling
Research & trading — validated or killed both count
Hypothesis · Data · Backtest · Risk · Execution · Calibration · Infra · Reporting
Compliance & regulated — outcome = audit evidence
Policy · Audit · Validation · Documentation · Training · CAPA · Risk · Submission
Yours — board ⚙ → Categories
any comma-separated list, ≤ 24 entries — items never get recoded

Saved views

Every vertical slices its board differently. ☆ Save view captures the current search + category + label filters as a named chip on the toolbar — click to apply, click again to clear, ✕ to delete. Stored in project.json, so views travel with the data.

Agent-readable, like everything else

$ curl -s localhost:4789/api/insights | jq '{projects:.projectCount, risks:(.risks|length),
    p50:.forecast.forecast.p50Date, initiatives:(.initiatives|length)}'

GET /api/insights · GET /api/projects/:id/insights — anything a user can see on the Insights pages, an agent can read and act on. The handoff loop can now prioritize by risk, not just by score.
